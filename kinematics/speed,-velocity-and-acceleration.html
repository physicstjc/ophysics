Speed, Velocity and Acceleration

Speed

*️⃣ Speed is the distance traveled per unit of time.

Speed measures how fast an object changes its position over time, typically expressed as the distance traveled per unit of time. It quantifies the rate of movement through space, reflecting how quickly an object is moving. Speed is a scalar quantity, meaning it only has magnitude and no directional component.

Displacement

Displacement is a vector that refers to the change in the position of an object.

Velocity

*️⃣ Velocity is the rate of change of displacement.

Velocity goes beyond speed by encompassing both the magnitude and direction of an object's motion, depicting how fast it travels in a particular direction through space.

Acceleration

*️⃣ Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity.

Acceleration signifies the rate of change of an object's velocity over time, illustrating how quickly its speed and/or direction of motion is altering.
