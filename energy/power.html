Power

Definition

*️⃣ Power is defined as the rate of energy transfer.

In mathematical terms, it is expressed as:

$$P = \dfrac{E}{t}$$ where $P$ is the power,
$E$ is the energy transferred, and
$t$ is the time taken

The standard unit of power is the watt (W), where one watt equals one joule of work done per second (1 W = 1 J/s). For example, if a person does 100 joules of work in 20 seconds, the power exerted is 5.0 watts.

Check for Understanding

An elevator has a mass of 1000 kg and carries a maximum load of 800 kg. Determine the minimum power that the motor must deliver to lift the fully loaded elevator at a constant speed of 3.0 m s-1.

Given that elevator is travelling at constant speed, this means that the work done by the motor is equal to the gain in gravitational potential energy of the elevator and the load.

$P = \dfrac{E}{t} = \dfrac{mgh}{t}$

$\>\>\>\>= 1800 \text{ kg} \times 10 \text{ N kg}^{-1} \times 3.0 \text{ m s}^{-1}$

$\>\>\>\>= 53 000 \text{ W}$
